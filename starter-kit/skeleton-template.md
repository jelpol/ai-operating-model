# The seven-part skeleton (template)

Every AI-managed domain of work (a job search, a business function, an
advisory practice, a compliance program) gets built from this frame. Fill
in each part for your domain. Deviations are allowed and encouraged where
your context genuinely differs, but they must be NAMED with a reason, never
silently skipped. The word "partially" is never allowed to pass silently.

## 1. Lenses

The perspectives the AI applies WHILE building the work, not just when
checking it afterward. Name three to five that fit your domain.

## 3. Rubric

How the work gets graded before it ships. The grade and the found
weaknesses are presented WITH the work, never silently absorbed into it,
and shipped work gets retrospective critique on a cadence. A review that
produces no findings is incomplete, not praise.

Three minimums. Every number that drives a decision carries its value,
date, and source, marked actual or estimate. Nothing outbound skips a final
pre-flight check. Every session starts by reconciling against the last

## 5. Learning loop

When a correction or a finished piece teaches you something, it gets
written into the right file so the next piece of work is better. Every
lesson has a named destination, and some destinations live outside the
workspace (a cross-domain inbox, a shared register), so nothing learned
anywhere is forgotten everywhere.

## 6. Stated limit

One honest sentence about what this system cannot do. The standard form:
one model wearing all the hats is not independent review; real data and the
human's correction are the strongest checks. Write yours down. It is the
sentence that makes the rest believable.

## 7. Verification gate

Anything vendor, version, price, or time dependent gets verified against a
live source before it is stated, never from the model's memory. Claims
carry a confidence label: established fact, consensus practice, or judgment
call. High-stakes work gets a separate adversarial pass built to refute it.
The gate covers approaches as well as facts: before inventing a novel
mechanism, search how established practice already solves it, and credit
what you borrow. The same gates apply to changes to this system itself.
